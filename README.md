# Loan Evaluation Process – BPMN Diagram

This repository contains a BPMN diagram modeling the workflow of a loan evaluation process in a banking environment.

The goal of this diagram is to illustrate how different actors interact during the evaluation of a loan application, from submission by the client to the final approval or rejection decision.

## Process Overview

The process is divided into four main actors:

- **Client** – Submits the loan application.
- **Loan Evaluation System** – Collects financial documents and calculates the risk score.
- **Credit Analyst** – Reviews and analyzes the financial data provided by the client.
- **Approval Committee** – Evaluates whether the risk level is acceptable and decides whether to approve or reject the loan.

## Workflow Steps

1. The client submits a loan application.
2. The system collects the required financial documents.
3. A credit analyst analyzes the financial data.
4. The system calculates a risk score.
5. The approval committee evaluates whether the risk is acceptable.
6. The loan is either approved or rejected.

## Purpose

This diagram demonstrates business process modeling using **BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation)**, a standard widely used by Business Analysts to represent workflows, decision points, and responsibilities across different actors.
